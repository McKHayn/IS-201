EMS Events

Events and Mock MCI

BYU's Mock Mass Casualty Incident (MCI) exercises are critical components of the university's emergency preparedness strategy. These simulations, meticulously planned and executed by BYU's Emergency Medical Services (EMS) team, replicate large-scale emergencies to evaluate and enhance the institution's response capabilities. Through realistic scenarios involving multiple casualties, emergency responders, and various medical challenges, participants gain invaluable hands-on experience in triage, treatment, and coordination. These exercises not only sharpen the skills of medical personnel but also foster collaboration among different departments and agencies, ensuring a cohesive and effective response in the event of a real emergency.

At the heart of BYU's vibrant sports culture, the presence of BYU EMS is as integral as the cheers from the crowd. During high-energy events like football and basketball games, BYU EMS stands ready on the sidelines, equipped to handle any medical situations that may arise. Whether it's attending to minor injuries, providing medical assessments, or coordinating with other emergency services, the team's dedication to ensuring the safety and well-being of athletes, coaches, spectators, and staff is unwavering. Their swift and efficient response allows the games to proceed smoothly, creating an environment where fans can immerse themselves in the excitement of the game with peace of mind.

Within the serene campus grounds of BYU, emergencies can occur unexpectedly, and when they do, BYU EMS springs into action. Responding to calls ranging from medical emergencies to accidents, the EMS team is the first line of defense in providing immediate assistance to those in need. Whether it's administering first aid, stabilizing patients, or coordinating with additional medical resources, BYU EMS personnel exemplify professionalism and compassion in their response efforts. Their round-the-clock presence ensures that students, faculty, and visitors can navigate the campus confidently, knowing that help is readily available in times of crisis.
